Mitarashi Kushi Dango

Ingredients Directions
1 1/3 cup joshinko (rice flour) Combine shiratamako, joshinko, and 2 tbsp. sugar in a bowl.
1 1/3 cup shiratamako/mochiko (Sweet Rice /Glutinous Rice Flour) Gradually pour hot water over the combination. Stir in hot water a little bit a time and knead slowly until the dough becomes smooth.
3/4 cup hot water Mix it using a spatula until combined.
2/3 cup water Knead the dough with hands until smooth.
1/2 cup sugar Divide the dough into small pieces and roll into small balls.
2 1/2 tbsp. soy sauce Place the dumplings in a preheated steamer and steam them on high heat for about 10 minutes.
1 1/2 tbsp. katakuriko starch or cornstarch, mixed with 1 1/2 tbsp. of water Cool the dumplings and put dumplings on skewers. (3 or 4 dumplings per skewer)
5 bamboo skewers To make mitarashi sauce, mix water, sugar, and soy sauce in a sauce pan.
Bring to a boil over medium heat.
Pour the katakuriko starch mixture into the sauce, stirring well.
Bring to a boil again and stop the heat.
Grill the skewered dumplings until slightly grill-marked.
Brush the mitarashi sauce over dumplings.

Japanese Rice Ball

Ingredients Directions
2 cups uncooked japanese short grain rice Put the rice in a large bowl and gently wash the rice in a circular motion and discard the water. Repeat this process about 3-4 times.
2 1/2 cups water Let the rice soak in water for 30 minutes. Transfer the rice into a sieve and drain completely, at least 15 minutes.
Kosher salt Combine the rice and water in a heavy-bottom pot (See Notes). Cover the lid and bring it to a boil over medium heat.
4 sheets of nori (seaweed) Once water is boiling, turn the heat to the lowest setting and continue to cook covered for 12 to 13 minutes, or until the water is completely absorbed. At 12-13 minute mark, take a quick peek and if you see there is any water left, close the lid and continue cooking for another minute or so.
Salted salmon (1 fillet salmon) + (Kosher salt) Remove the pot (with the lid on) from the heat and let it steam for another 10 minutes. Then transfer the rice to a large plate (I use Sushi Oke). Fluff the rice with a rice scooper. Let the cooked rice cool a little bit until you can hold rice without burning your hands. However, do not let the rice completely cool down.
While rice is being soaked and drained (45 minutes), prepare the onigiri fillings. Salted salmon filling: Sprinkle kosher salt on both sides of the salmon fillet. Bake at 400 ºF (200 ºC) degrees in a toaster oven or oven for 25 minutes.
Break the cooked salmon into flakes and set aside.
Cut the nori sheets in thirds (1/3).
First wet both of your hands with water in order to keep the rice from sticking to your hands.
Then put some salt in your hands and rub to spread all around your palms. How much salt? I dip 3 finger tips in kosher salt shown in the picture below. If you are using table salt, use half amount as it’s saltier than kosher salt.
Scoop out a handful of warm rice (about 1/3 cup) into one hand. Create a small well (indentation) in the center of the rice. Put one kind of fillings (about 1-2 tsp.) inside. Then mold the rice with your hands around the well to cover your filling completely.
Press the rice around the filling to gently form the rice into a triangle. I use three fingers (thumb, index finger, middle finger) to make a triangle corner. Your hands should be just firm enough so the onigiri doesn't fall apart. You don't want to squeeze the rice too tight.
Wrap the onigiri with nori (seaweed).

Apple Harvest Pound Cake With Caramel Glaze

Ingredients Directions
2 cups white sugar Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 9 inch Bundt pan.
1 1/2 cups vegetable oil In a large bowl, beat the sugar, oil, vanilla and eggs with an electric mixer until light and fluffy. Combine the flour, baking soda, cinnamon and salt; stir into the batter just until blended. Fold in the apples and walnuts using a spoon. Pour into the prepared pan.
2 teaspoons vanilla extract Bake for 1 hour and 20 minutes in the preheated oven, until a toothpick inserted into the crown or the cake comes out clean. Allow to cool for about 20 minutes then invert on to a wire rack.
3 eggs Make the glaze by heating the butter, milk and brown sugar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, stirring to dissolve the sugar, then remove from the heat. Drizzle over the warm cake. I like to place a sheet of aluminum foil under the cooling rack to catch the drips for easy clean up.
3 cups all-purpose flour
1 teaspoon baking soda
1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
1 teaspoon salt
2 medium Granny Smith apples - peeled, cored and chopped
1 cup chopped walnuts
1/2 cup butter or margarine
2 teaspoons milk
1/2 cup brown sugar
